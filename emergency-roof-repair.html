Not every roof problem can wait for a standard appointment. Active water intrusion, storm-torn shingles or tile, and debris impacts from falling limbs can escalate fast, soaking insulation and drywall within hours. When that's happening, the priority is stopping the water, not scheduling a routine inspection three weeks out.

We don't promise guaranteed same-day or 24/7 dispatch — that's not a claim we're willing to make without being able to back it up every time. What we can tell you honestly: call us, describe what's happening, and we'll let you know what's realistically possible given our current schedule and the weather. If we can get to you sooner, we will.

When to Call Now

Situations that can't wait.

Active Leak During Rain
Water actively coming through the ceiling or attic during a storm is the clearest sign the situation needs attention now, not later.
Storm or Wind Damage
Torn-off shingles, cracked tile, or a section of underlayment exposed directly to the sky after high winds.
Fallen Branch or Debris Impact
A direct impact can puncture roofing material even when the damage isn't obvious from the ground.
Loose or Lifted Roofing Section
If part of the roof looks like it's peeling back or has shifted, continued wind or rain can make it worse quickly.
What Happens Next

What we can realistically do.

Call and describe the situation: what's happening, where, and whether water is actively getting in. We'll be straight with you about timing based on our current schedule and the weather — not a scripted promise. If a full repair can't happen the same day, we'll talk through whether temporary protection like tarping makes sense in the meantime, and get a permanent repair scheduled as soon as reasonably possible.

Common Questions

Got questions?

Do you offer 24/7 emergency roofing service?+
We don't advertise round-the-clock guaranteed dispatch. Call to check urgent repair availability — depending on our schedule and current conditions, we may be able to get to an active leak or storm damage sooner than a standard appointment.
What counts as a roofing emergency?+
An active leak during rain, a tree limb or debris impact that's broken through the roofing material, wind damage that's exposed the underlayment, or a section of roof that's come loose are the situations worth calling about right away rather than waiting for a routine estimate.
Can you tarp a damaged roof temporarily?+
In many cases, yes. A temporary tarp or cover can reduce further water intrusion until a permanent repair can be scheduled. Ask about this when you call.
Will you have to guess at a repair cost over the phone?+
No. We'll ask enough questions to understand the urgency and likely scope, but any real estimate comes from inspecting the roof in person.
