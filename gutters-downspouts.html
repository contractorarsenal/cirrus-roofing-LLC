Gutters and Downspouts

Gutters and Downspouts in
Watsonville, CA.

Installation, repair and drainage work that protects the roof edge, fascia and foundation — not just an afterthought to the roof itself.

Gutters do more work than most homeowners give them credit for. Properly sized, clean gutters move water away from the roof edge, fascia boards and foundation before it has a chance to cause damage. When they're clogged, undersized, or pulling away from the fascia, that water finds somewhere else to go — often back up under the roofing material or straight down against the foundation.

Across Watsonville, Santa Cruz, Scotts Valley and other tree-covered properties in this region, gutters fill with organic debris faster than in open, low-canopy areas. That makes gutter condition and drainage capacity worth checking on a regular basis, not just when water is visibly overflowing.

Why It's Connected to the Roof

Not a separate problem.

Backed-up gutters can push water up and under the roofing material at the edge, which is a common but often overlooked contributor to leaks that seem to come from the roof itself. If you're dealing with a leak near the roof edge or fascia, it's worth having both the roof and the gutters checked together — see roof leak repair if that's what's going on.

Common Questions

Got questions?

How often should gutters be cleaned in this area?+
Properties under redwood or oak canopy typically need more frequent clearing than open properties, since falling debris accumulates faster. We can advise on a reasonable schedule for your specific property during an inspection.
What happens if gutters are left clogged?+
Water backs up under the roof edge, can seep behind fascia boards, and in cold enough conditions can contribute to ice damming. Overflowing gutters near the foundation can also lead to drainage problems around the home.
Do you repair sections of gutter, or only replace the whole system?+
Both — a damaged or detached section can often be repaired or replaced on its own without redoing the whole system, provided the rest of the gutters are in reasonable shape.
Can gutter problems actually damage the roof itself?+
Yes. Water that backs up because of clogged or undersized gutters can work its way up under the roof edge and shingles, which is why gutter condition is worth checking alongside the roof itself, not as a separate afterthought.
